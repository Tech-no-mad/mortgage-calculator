The Federal Housing Administration (FHA) sets specific maximum borrowing limits for every county in the United States, and Travis County, Texas is no exception. If you are looking to buy a home in Austin or the surrounding areas using an FHA loan in 2026, understanding these limits is the first crucial step in your homebuying journey.

### 2026 FHA Loan Limits for Travis County, TX

For 2026, the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) has established the following FHA loan limits for Travis County based on property size:

*   **1-Unit (Single-Family):** $531,300
*   **2-Unit (Duplex):** $680,150
*   **3-Unit (Triplex):** $822,150
*   **4-Unit (Fourplex):** $1,021,700

These numbers represent a significant increase from previous years, reflecting the rising property values across the Austin metro area. 

### Why the Travis County FHA Limit Matters

FHA loans are incredibly popular because they require a down payment as low as 3.5% and have much more forgiving credit score requirements (down to 580) compared to conventional loans. However, you cannot borrow a single dollar over the $531,300 limit for a single-family home. 

If you find a home in Travis County for $550,000, you cannot simply use an FHA loan for $531,300 and finance the rest. The loan amount *must* be under the limit, meaning any gap between the home price (minus your 3.5% down payment) and the FHA limit must be covered by you in cash, or you must switch to a conventional or Jumbo loan.

### The True Cost: Travis County Property Taxes + FHA MIP

When calculating your monthly payment, do not just look at the principal and interest. 

**1. Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP):** FHA loans require both an upfront MIP (1.75% of the loan amount) and an annual MIP (typically 0.55% of the loan balance, divided by 12 and added to your monthly payment).

**2. Travis County Property Taxes:** This is the hidden trap. The average effective property tax rate in Travis County is approximately **1.76%**. This is significantly higher than the national average and will drastically affect your monthly payment.

If you max out the single-family limit and borrow exactly $531,300 at a 6.5% interest rate, your base Principal & Interest payment is roughly **$3,358/mo**. 
However, when you add the estimated $779/mo in Travis County property taxes, $150/mo for homeowners insurance, and $243/mo for FHA MIP, your *true* monthly payment jumps to over **$4,530/mo**.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use an FHA loan to buy a home over the limit in Travis County?**
No. The FHA will not insure a loan amount greater than the county limit. If the home price minus your down payment exceeds the limit, you must bring the difference in cash or use a conventional loan.

**What credit score do I need for FHA in Texas?**
To qualify for the maximum 96.5% financing (a 3.5% down payment), you need a minimum credit score of 580. If your score is between 500 and 579, you are required to put down at least 10%.

**Does Travis County have down payment assistance programs?**
Yes! Organizations like the Texas State Affordable Housing Corporation (TSAHC) and the Austin Housing Finance Corporation offer grants and deferred-forgivable loans to help cover the 3.5% down payment for first-time homebuyers using FHA loans in Travis County.
